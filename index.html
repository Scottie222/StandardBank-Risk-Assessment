BREACH TIMELINE - EXPERIAN SA 2020
24 + 27 MAY 2020
Fraudster obtains data from Experian
Fraudster posing as a company director submitted fraudulent data inquiry. Experian handed over 24M personal records without adequate verification.
22 JULY 2020
Experian discovers the fraud
58 days after data was handed over. No monitoring or anomaly detection flagged the fraudulent inquiry. Discovery was reactive.
19 AUGUST 2020
SABRIC public announcement
Standard Bank confirms client data exposed. CRO Piet Swanepoel: credit customers impacted due to mandatory bureau disclosure requirements.
1 SEPTEMBER 2020
Data found on public file sharing site
Breach data discovered on WeSendIt, a public file sharing platform. Confirms data was actively distributed.
SEPTEMBER 2021
Suspect arrested by Hawks in Gauteng
Directorate for Priority Crime Investigation arrests suspect. Criminal prosecution under Cybercrimes Act 19 of 2020.
OCTOBER 2021
Data re-emerges on Telegram
Stolen data resurfaced on Telegram channels. Demonstrates ongoing risk even after incident response and arrest.

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS - WHY THE BREACH SUCCEEDED
EXPERIAN SA - CONTROL FAILURES
Inadequate customer verification
Experian failed to verify the identity of the entity requesting data. A fraudster posing as a company director passed verification with no additional checks. ISO A.5.17 failure.
No anomaly detection on data queries
Single fraudulent request for 24M records went undetected. No monitoring or alerting for unusual query volumes. ISO A.8.16 failure.
No data minimisation
Full dataset returned rather than minimum necessary data. POPIA Section 9 and GDPR Article 5(c) both require data minimisation. Direct POPIA violation.
Weak third-party access controls
Data inquiry process had insufficient controls for bulk data access. No requirement for stepped-up authentication for large volume requests.
58-day detection gap
Fraud occurred on 24 May. Discovered on 22 July. 58 days of undetected exposure. No real-time monitoring of data access patterns.
STANDARD BANK - SYSTEMIC EXPOSURE
Standard Bank was a victim of Experian's control failures, but the breach exposed systemic gaps in how Standard Bank managed its third-party data processor relationships.
POPIA S.21
Operator contracts did not adequately bind Experian to equivalent security standards.
POPIA S.19
Insufficient oversight of security safeguards at the credit bureau processing personal information.
ISO A.5.19
No evidence of regular supplier security assessments or penetration testing requirements.
ISO A.5.22
Monitoring of supplier information security practices was insufficient or absent.

RECOMMENDED CONTROLS - ISO 27001 ANNEX A
IMMEDIATE - THIRD PARTY RISK
ROOT CAUSE CONTROLS
Enhanced supplier due diligence
Annual security assessments of all credit bureaus. Penetration testing requirements. ISO A.5.19 / POPIA S.21
Contractual security obligations
Bind all data processors to ISO 27001-equivalent controls. POPIA operator agreements. ISO A.5.20
Stepped-up authentication for bulk queries
Require additional verification for requests above defined volume thresholds. ISO A.5.17
Continuous supplier monitoring
Real-time monitoring of data access patterns at credit bureaus. ISO A.5.22
30 DAYS - DETECTION AND RESPONSE
DETECTION GAP CONTROLS
SIEM with bureau integration
Centralised logging of all credit bureau data access events. Alert on anomalous volumes. ISO A.8.16
Tested incident response plan
Tabletop exercise for third-party breach scenario. POPIA S.22 notification procedure. ISO A.5.24
Customer fraud monitoring uplift
Enhanced transaction monitoring for affected 24M customers. Proactive fraud alerts. ISO A.8.16
Identity verification strengthening
MFA for all credit-related account access. Biometric verification for high-risk transactions. ISO A.8.5
90 DAYS - GOVERNANCE
SYSTEMIC CONTROLS
Information Security Policy update
Integrate third-party data processor requirements. Board-level approval. ISO Clause 5.2
Data classification framework
Classify credit bureau data as critical. Apply data minimisation controls. POPIA S.9 / ISO A.5.12
POPIA compliance programme
Annual POPIA compliance review. Information Officer appointment. PAIA/POPIA alignment.
Vulnerability assessment programme
Quarterly vulnerability scans of systems processing credit bureau data. ISO A.8.8

REGULATORY FRAMEWORK SUMMARY
POPIA Act 4 of 2013
Primary data protection law. Maximum fine R10M or 10% of annual turnover. Imprisonment up to 10 years for egregious violations. Information Regulator has enforcement authority.
National Credit Act - legal basis for exposure
NCA requires credit providers to submit customer credit data to registered credit bureaus. This is why Standard Bank's data was at Experian — it is a legal obligation, not optional.
Cybercrimes Act 19 of 2020
Criminal prosecution of the fraudster who obtained the data. Demonstrates SA's expanding legal framework for cybercrime prosecution.
SARB Prudential Authority notification
Experian notified banking regulator as part of breach response. Demonstrates intersection of data protection and financial sector regulation in SA.
